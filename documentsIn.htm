Parking basics
Type A • Each unit has one assigned, yellow numbered, parking space. Do not park in a numbered slot that is not yours.
Type B • Yellow striped, unnumbered parking spaces are available for registered vehicles with properly displayed parking tags.
Type C • Green striped parking spaces are available for guests. Parking tags are required at all times. Green striped parking spaces are located between Visitor Parking signs.
On snow days, when our crew is clearing your lot, parking spaces must be emptied.
  • A snow day is any day where snow fall exceeds 1.5 inches.
  • Lot clearing can occur at any time between 7:30 am and 4:00 pm.
  • Failure to move your car will result in fines.
Dysfunctional or Inoperable vehicles will be removed.

FAQ: Parking.
Q • I do not have a car. My assigned parking spot is open. Can a person visiting me park there? Over night too?
A • You may offer your assigned parking slot to any vehicle with any valid parking sticker or tag that is properly displayed.
 
Q • I have two cars. They both have valid parking stickers. All the unnumbered yellow parking spaces are taken. Can I park in a green visitor slot? Over night too?
A • A vehicle with a parking sticker can park in a green visitor slot after 10:00 pm but must be moved by 7:00 am. Remember, this area is reserved for visitors. Please consider locating an appropriate parking space in another Coach Light parking lot.
 
Q • I have a care giver that visits several times a week. Where should that person park?
A • Visit with the office and secure a parking tag. The tag can be kept in your home. The care giver can park in a visitors slot, pick up the parking tag, display it properly, and return it to you before they leave.
 
Q • How do I properly display a parking tag?
A • If your parking tag is a sticker, Please attach the sticker on the inside of the front window, passengers side, lower right had corner. All other parking tags will hang freely from your rear-view mirror.
 
Q • How do I get a parking tag?
A • There are one year parking stickers, one month parking stickers, and one day/night parking tags. Visit the office, bring proof of residence. When applying for a sticker, bring vehicle registration and auto insurance card.
 
Q • Are there parking tag fees?
A • A package of One Day / Over Night parking tags are available for $?.??
 
Q • How long can a guest stay before I need to make special parking arrangements?
A • One Day / Over Night parking tags can be used for 3 days before vehicle needs special parking registration.

Things To Know About Pets

No animal may be kept which the Board has determined to be dangerous or which has been designated as dangerous by any governmental agency. More >>

Pet Rules and Restrictions
• The housing of pets is subject to termination by the Board of Directors if the pet becomes a nuisance or the pet’s owner does not follow the rules regarding pets as listed here in.
• New owners or tenants shall be limited to one dog or cat, 30lbs. or less. If a present owner or tenant has more than one per unit, the deceased pet cannot be replaced.
• The following dog breeds require Board approval: Pit bull or Staffordshire terrier, Rottweiler, German Shepherd, Husky, Malamute, Doberman Pinscher, Chow Chow, Great Dane, St. Bernard, Wolf mix
• Home owners are prohibited from leaving food outside their unit. We live near a forest preserve and near wet lands. Your pet supplies will be consumed by skunks, raccoons, and coyotes. These wild animals pose a danger to the community. You have an obligation to your neighbors first.
• No animal shall be kept, bred or maintained:
  • For any commercial purpose.
  • In unreasonable numbers.
  • That would emanate any odor.
  • That creates excessive noise.
• The pet owner is responsible for picking up after their pet. The pet owner is responsible if a pet walker is negligent.
• Pets must be kept on a leash and under proper control when they are outside the apartments.
• No pet may be left in an apartment, unattended, for any period of time if the pet creates a nuisance to other residents.
• Use specific areas that are designated as 'Pet Walks'.
• Pets are not permitted in inner common areas. Example: storage or Basement.

Our Balconies

Balconies: Rules and Restrictions
• All charcoal and liquid starter are strictly prohibited. Only gas propane grills are allowed.
• All patios and balconies are to be free of all items except barbecue, lawn furniture and live plants (only 3 small pots).
• Every 10th of each month, the maintenance department will check all balconies and patios. They will put in the garbage anything that does not belong on the balconies or patios.
• Do not store things on balconies that will inhibit access to or egress.
• During early fall, remove all the plants and toys from railings and wood area.
• No running, ride bikes, or roller skate, Or engage in unruly conduct.
• Stack the lawn chairs and put them securely against the wall.
• Cover the grill.
• Please keep this area clean at all times.
